Fun Facts

🧱 The mortar used in the Great Wall of China was made with sticky rice.

🌹 The city of Petra is also known as the 'Rose City' due to the color of its stone.

🏟️ The Colosseum in Rome could hold an estimated 50,000 to 80,000 spectators.

🏔️ Machu Picchu was unknown to the outside world until 1911.

🎨 The Taj Mahal appears to change color depending on the time of day.

⚡ Christ the Redeemer is struck by lightning on average 3-6 times a year.

🐍 Chichen Itza's pyramid has 365 steps, and a serpent shadow appears during the equinoxes.

📏 The Great Pyramid of Giza was the tallest man-made structure for over 3,800 years.

🤔 The Hanging Gardens of Babylon are the only Ancient Wonder whose existence is disputed.

💡 The Lighthouse of Alexandria was said to be visible from 35 miles away.

🐘 The Statue of Zeus at Olympia was crafted from precious ivory and gold.

🏛️ The Temple of Artemis at Ephesus was rebuilt at least three times before its final destruction.

⚰️ The Mausoleum at Halicarnassus was so grand it gave us the word 'mausoleum'.

☀️ The Colossus of Rhodes was a giant statue of the Greek sun-god Helios.

🔪 The stones of Machu Picchu are so perfectly fitted that a knife blade cannot pass between them.

🎬 The Treasury at Petra was a filming location for 'Indiana Jones and the Last Crusade'.

☂️ The Colosseum had a retractable awning called a 'velarium' to shield spectators from the sun.

📐 The Taj Mahal's four minarets lean slightly outwards to protect the tomb during an earthquake.

👨‍🎨 The face of Christ the Redeemer was sculpted by Romanian artist Gheorghe Leonida.

🧭 The Great Pyramid of Giza is aligned to true north with remarkable precision.

💧 The ancient city of Petra had a sophisticated water management system of dams and cisterns.

🌊 The Romans flooded the Colosseum to stage spectacular mock naval battles.

🗣️ The name "Machu Picchu" means "Old Peak" in the local Quechua language.

⚖️ The Taj Mahal is perfectly symmetrical, except for the sarcophagus of Shah Jahan himself.

🏗️ The construction of Christ the Redeemer took nine years, from 1922 to 1931.

⛓️ The Great Wall is not one continuous wall but a system of walls and fortifications.

🧱 The Colosseum was built with over 100,000 cubic meters of travertine stone.

🏠 Machu Picchu consists of more than 150 buildings, from houses to temples.

💎 The Taj Mahal is inlaid with 28 types of precious and semi-precious jewels.

⚖️ The Christ the Redeemer statue weighs as much as 350 cars (635 metric tons).

💀 The 'Cenote Sagrado' at Chichen Itza was a sacred sinkhole used for human sacrifices.

🪨 Petra is mentioned in the Bible under the name 'Sela', which also means rock.

⏳ The Colosseum was actively used for entertainment for about 390 years.

☀️ Machu Picchu's Intihuatana stone was a precise astronomical clock or calendar.

👷 Over 20,000 artisans and craftsmen were employed to build the Taj Mahal.

🎨 Christ the Redeemer is the largest Art Deco-style sculpture in the world.

🏀 The ball court at Chichen Itza is the largest in the Americas, bigger than a modern football field.

✍️ The Incas who built Machu Picchu had no written language, using knotted strings called 'quipu' instead.

🚪 The entrance to Petra is through the Siq, a narrow gorge over 1km long and 80m high.

⏱️ The Colosseum could be evacuated of 50,000 people in just a few minutes thanks to its 80 exits.

A Digital Museum

Welcome to WOW, our group's ambitious project to chronicle the most awe-inspiring sights on our planet. This website is a living testament to what can be built with the foundational language of the web: pure HTML. Here, we explore the magical convergence of history, architecture, and nature.

Our theme covers the celebrated New7Wonders of the World, which are human-made marvels, and touches upon breathtaking natural wonders that leave us humbled. This is more than a list; it is an invitation to explore.
